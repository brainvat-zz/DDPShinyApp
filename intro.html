Blogger Antonio S. Chinchón (@aschinchon), who posted an interesting article "Odd Connections Inside The NASDAQ-100" on his Ripples blog on May 8, 2015, noticed some companies on the NASDAQ tend to see their stock prices rise and fall together even though the firms might be in very different industries. We wonder, if two companies' stocks follow each other, can we use predictions about changes in one stock to predict the other?

In this Shiny app, we adapt a change point detection methodology illustrated by Roberto Rösler in his January 1, 2015 article, "Change Point Detection in Time Series with R and Tableau" to help us explore whether this is possible.
